Glossary: AI Evaluation & Compliance

Important terms from our field explained understandably:

Fine-Tuning

Adaptation of a pre-trained AI model for specific tasks. Requires separate evaluation as behavior can fundamentally change.

OOD (Out-of-Distribution)

Inputs outside the training data that can influence model behavior. Critical for safety as unpredictable behavior can occur.

Red Teaming

Simulation of attack scenarios, such as jailbreaks, to identify security vulnerabilities.

EU AI Act

European regulation for AI systems. Penalties up to 7% of global revenue possible.

DIN SPEC 92005

German standard for handling quantifiable and non-quantifiable uncertainties of AI systems.

Jailbreak

Circumvention of restrictions intended by the operator of an AI model through prompts optimized for this purpose with manipulated formulations.

AI Router

System that automatically selects the optimal AI model for a given task based on performance, cost, and compliance criteria.

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

AI approach that combines information retrieval with text generation to produce more accurate and context-aware responses.

Adversarial Attacks

Specially crafted inputs designed to cause AI models to make mistakes or produce unexpected outputs.

Multi-Agent System

Network of multiple AI agents that work together to solve complex problems beyond the capability of individual agents.

Benchmarking

Systematic comparison of AI system performance against standardized tests and metrics.

Compliance Risk

Potential for legal penalties, financial forfeiture, or material loss an organization faces when it fails to act in accordance with industry laws and regulations.
